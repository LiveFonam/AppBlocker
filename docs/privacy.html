This Privacy Policy explains, in plain language, what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how it is used and shared, and the rights you have over it. It is written to comply with Canada's PIPEDA, Quebec's Law 25 (the Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector, as amended), California's CCPA/CPRA, and the U.S. federal COPPA.

The short version: We collect your school email for sign-in. Your blocked-apps list, daily limits, time blocks, usage stats, and block attempts all stay on your device. We do not sell your data, do not show ads, do not run analytics, and do not track you across other apps for advertising.

7. Cross-Border Transfer of Personal Information

Supabase, Expo, and GitHub are all based in the United States. When you sign in, your email, session token, and your block settings are processed in the U.S.

Under Quebec Law 25 §17, we must assess whether sending data outside Quebec gives it adequate protection. We have considered:

  • Necessity: Authentication is core to the Service and there is no Quebec-based provider that offers an equivalent secure, school-email OTP service.
  • Proportionality: Only the minimum needed (email + token) is sent.
  • Security: All transit is encrypted (HTTPS). Data at rest at Supabase is encrypted. Sessions are stored on your device using OS-level encrypted storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • Legal risk: U.S. law (including the CLOUD Act) may allow lawful-access requests by U.S. authorities. We will challenge any overbroad request to the extent legally possible.

By using the Service, you acknowledge this cross-border transfer.

12. Security

We take security seriously, but no system is perfect.

  • All network traffic is over HTTPS / TLS.
  • Your authentication session is stored on your device in encrypted secure storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore) via expo-secure-store.
  • Your settings, email, and counters are stored using your device's standard app storage (AsyncStorage). This is sandboxed to the app and not encrypted at rest by us, but the data never leaves your device.
  • At Supabase, data is encrypted at rest using AES-256.

If a security incident affects your data, we will notify the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec and affected users within 72 hours of becoming aware, where required by Quebec Law 25.

13. iOS Screen Time & Android Usage Access

To enforce limits and show you real stats, the Service needs to know how long you have spent in other apps. This is read from your device's system APIs only after you grant the appropriate permission:

  • iOS: Apple's FamilyControls, ManagedSettings, and DeviceActivity frameworks (collectively, the Screen Time API). You will see Apple's own permission prompt the first time we ask. The list of apps you choose to block is selected via Apple's system FamilyActivityPicker, which returns an opaque, on-device token. We never see the names or bundle identifiers of the apps you blocked — only a token Apple's framework uses to apply the shield. ManagedSettings then displays Apple's shield over those apps when a focus session is active. You can revoke the permission any time from Settings → Screen Time → Apps that Can Access Screen Time or directly within Settings > Student Focus.
  • Android: The Usage Access permission and Accessibility Service. The Accessibility Service runs in the background and watches for window-state-change events; when the foreground app matches your blocked list it launches Student Focus on top to redirect you. You grant both permissions manually in Android Settings and can revoke either at any time.

This data is read on your device and used on your device. The opaque app-selection token, your daily-usage counters, and your block decisions stay on your device. They are not sent to our servers and are not visible to us.

14. Friend Control (Optional Feature)

If you enable Friend Control, you can ask one trusted friend to be your accountability partner. When you do:

  • What we share: Your randomly generated 10-character invite code, and a single row in our friend_pairings table that links your Supabase user ID to the friend's Supabase user ID once they accept. Approval and revocation timestamps are written when you tap Approve or Reject on an incoming request.
  • What the friend sees: Only the existence of a pairing with you (the row's existence) and a rotating 6-digit override code that is computed locally on their device from the shared invite code using a one-way hash. The friend does NOT see your blocked-apps list, your stats, your email, or any of your usage data. They cannot read your other rows.
  • What you see: Pending and historical pairing requests in your "Incoming requests" inbox, including the last four characters of the friend's Supabase user ID and the request timestamp. You do not see the friend's email or any other personal data about them.
  • How rotating codes work: The 6-digit override codes are derived locally on both phones from the same shared invite code plus the current hour. No code, attempt, or successful override is ever sent to our servers. The verification happens entirely on your phone.
  • Real-time updates: When a friend claims your invite code, Supabase's real-time channel notifies your phone so the inbox banner appears immediately. The notification contains only the changed row.
  • Revoking: Reject or revoke a pairing in your inbox at any time. The row is marked revoked_at and the friend can no longer generate valid override codes.
  • Deleting: When you delete your account, all rows where you are user_id or friend_user_id are deleted, regardless of approval state.

Friend Control is opt-in. You can use the app entirely without it (Self Control via 10-second hold + 4-minute waiting period).

15. Push Notifications

If you allow notifications, your device registers a local notification permission with the OS. Student Focus currently uses notifications only for local in-app prompts (for example, the Friend Control approval banner). We do not currently send remote push notifications, and no device token is uploaded to our servers. You can revoke notification permission at any time in your device's OS settings.

16. Account Deletion

You may delete your account and all associated data at any time:

  • In-app: Settings → "Delete my account". This signs you out, clears local data, and triggers server-side deletion of every row keyed to your Supabase user ID (block settings, friend pairings on either side, sessions). Your authentication record at Supabase is also removed.
  • By email: If you cannot access the in-app option, email livefonam@gmail.com from the account address and we will perform the same deletion within 30 days.

Account deletion is irreversible. We retain authentication logs (sign-in attempts, IP addresses) at Supabase only for the period required by their policy, after which they are aged out.
