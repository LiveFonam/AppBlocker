Privacy Policy

Effective: May 14, 2026 · Last updated: May 14, 2026

Student Focus ("we", "our", "us", or the "Service") is operated by an individual developer based in Quebec, Canada. This Privacy Policy explains, in plain language, what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how it is used and shared, and the rights you have over it. It is written to comply with Canada's PIPEDA, Quebec's Law 25 (the Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector, as amended), California's CCPA/CPRA, and the U.S. federal COPPA.

The short version: We collect your school email for sign-in. Your blocked-apps list, daily limits, time blocks, usage stats, and block attempts all stay on your device. We do not sell your data, do not show ads, do not run analytics, and do not track you across other apps for advertising.

  • We do not access your contacts, photos, camera, microphone, or precise location.

7. Cross-Border Transfer of Personal Information

Supabase, Expo, and GitHub are all based in the United States. When you sign in, your email and session token are processed in the U.S.

Under Quebec Law 25 §17, we must assess whether sending data outside Quebec gives it adequate protection. We have considered:

  • Necessity: Authentication is core to the Service and there is no Quebec-based provider that offers an equivalent secure, school-email OTP service.
  • Proportionality: Only the minimum needed (email + token) is sent.
  • Security: All transit is encrypted (HTTPS). Data at rest at Supabase is encrypted. Sessions are stored on your device using OS-level encrypted storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore).
  • Legal risk: U.S. law (including the CLOUD Act) may allow lawful-access requests by U.S. authorities. We will challenge any overbroad request to the extent legally possible.

By using the Service, you acknowledge this cross-border transfer.

9. Your Rights — California (CCPA / CPRA)

If you live in California you have:

  • Right to know what personal information we collect and how it is used.
  • Right to delete personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell or share for cross-context behavioral advertising. There is nothing to opt out of, but you have the right anyway.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not use sensitive PI for inferring characteristics, so this right is effectively already honored.
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not deny you the Service or charge a different price for exercising any of these rights.

To exercise any of these rights, email livefonam@gmail.com. We will verify your request (typically by confirming control of the email on file) and respond within 45 days as required by CCPA. An authorized agent may submit a request on your behalf with your written permission.

10. Data Retention

  • Your email and authentication session remain at Supabase while your account is active. When you ask us to delete your account, we delete the record within 30 days and instruct Supabase to do the same.
  • Local data on your device (settings, stats, attempt counters, PIN) is deleted as soon as you uninstall the app or use any "reset" action inside Settings.
  • Authentication logs at Supabase (sign-in attempts, IP addresses they may record) are retained per Supabase's policy.

11. Children (COPPA & Quebec Law 25 §14)

Student Focus is built for students aged 13 and older. We require an institutional school or university email address to sign up, which serves as a practical age gate because elementary schools generally do not issue such accounts to children under 13.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and you believe your child under 13 has created an account, please email us and we will delete it.

For Quebec residents aged 14 to 17: Quebec Law 25 permits a minor of 14 or older to provide their own consent for a service that is intended for them. Because Student Focus is targeted at students of high-school age and older, we treat your consent as valid.

12. Security

We take security seriously, but no system is perfect.

  • All network traffic is over HTTPS / TLS.
  • Your authentication session is stored on your device in encrypted secure storage (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore) via expo-secure-store.
  • Your settings, email, and counters are stored using your device's standard app storage (AsyncStorage). This is sandboxed to the app and not encrypted at rest by us — but the data never leaves your device.
  • At Supabase, data is encrypted at rest using AES-256.

If a security incident affects your data, we will notify the Commission d'accès à l'information du Québec and affected users within 72 hours of becoming aware, where required by Quebec Law 25.

13. iOS Screen Time & Android Usage Access

To enforce limits and show you real stats, the Service needs to know how long you have spent in other apps. This is read from your device's system APIs only after you grant the appropriate permission:

  • iOS: Apple's Screen Time / FamilyControls API. You will see Apple's own permission prompt the first time we ask. You can revoke it any time from Settings → Screen Time → See All App & Website Activity → Share Across Devices (path may vary by iOS version) or directly within Settings > Student Focus.
  • Android: The Usage Access permission and (optionally) Accessibility Service. You grant both manually in Android Settings.

This data is read on your device and used on your device. It is not sent to our servers and is not visible to us.

14. Push Notifications

If you allow notifications, your device registers a push token with Expo's push service (United States). The token is an anonymous device identifier used to deliver pushes; it cannot read information from your phone. You can revoke notification permission at any time in your device's OS settings.

If the change is material (for example, adding a new third-party service or starting to show ads), we will notify you inside the app before it takes effect, and you will have the opportunity to delete your account if you do not agree.
