6. How the Blocking Feature Works (Realism)

Blocking apps on a phone depends on permissions you grant and the operating system's own APIs:

  • iOS: Blocking is performed by Apple's FamilyControls / ManagedSettings / DeviceActivity frameworks (the Screen Time API). When you start a focus session, the App tells iOS to apply a shield to the apps you selected via Apple's system picker. Apple — not us — is what actually prevents the app from opening. If the entitlement is not active in a given build or you do not grant the system permission, the App's enforcement is limited to local UI hints (no real shield).
  • Android: Blocking is performed by an in-app Accessibility Service that detects when a blocked package comes to the foreground and immediately launches Student Focus on top. This is a redirect, not a true system lock — you can dismiss the Accessibility Service from Settings at any time, after which redirection stops. Requires the BIND_ACCESSIBILITY_SERVICE and PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS permissions, granted manually in Android Settings.

If those permissions are revoked or unavailable, the App cannot enforce blocks. We do our best, but we cannot guarantee a particular blocking outcome.
